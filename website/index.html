Why ArchiveJump exists

Quality journalism costs money, and we respect that. Publishers need to sustain their operations, pay their journalists, and continue producing important work.

But sometimes you hit a paywall when researching for school, fact-checking information, or trying to understand different perspectives on current events. Maybe you've already reached your monthly limit, or you're exploring whether a publication is worth subscribing to.

ArchiveJump helps in these moments by connecting you to archived versions that already exist on the public web. We're not creating new archives or circumventing protections‚Äîwe're simply making it easier to find content that's already been preserved by legitimate archival services.

üíô A note on ethics

If you regularly read a publication, please consider subscribing to support their work. ArchiveJump is best used for occasional research, fact-checking, or exploring new publications‚Äînot as a replacement for supporting journalism you value.

Questions & Support

Is this legal?

Yes. ArchiveJump only links to content that's already been archived by legitimate public archival services like Archive.ph and Wayback Machine.

Does it work on mobile?

Currently, ArchiveJump is a desktop browser extension. Mobile support is something we're considering for the future.

Which sites are supported?

Major news sites like NYT, WSJ, The Atlantic, Washington Post, The Guardian, Reuters, Bloomberg, and many more. See the full list in our documentation.

Is my data collected?

No. ArchiveJump stores your preferences locally on your device and doesn't send any data to external servers.

Can I contribute?

Absolutely! ArchiveJump is open source. You can report bugs, suggest features, or contribute code on our GitHub repository.

Store availability?

Mozilla Add-ons: submitted and currently under review. Chrome Web Store: in final preparation stages. Manual installation takes just 2 minutes and works perfectly while we wait for store approvals!

Still have questions?

Reach out on GitHub for support, bug reports, or feature requests. We're here to help!
